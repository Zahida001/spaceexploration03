Mercury

Did you know?

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun.

It has no atmosphere, leading to extreme temperature variations.

Venus

Did you know?

Venus has a thick atmosphere mostly made of carbon dioxide.

It's the hottest planet in the solar system, with surface temperatures hot enough to melt lead.

Earth

Did you know?

Earth is the only known planet to support life.

It has one natural satellite, the Moon, and is composed of a vast amount of water, mainly in oceans.

Mars

Did you know?

Mars is known as the Red Planet because of its reddish appearance.

It has the tallest volcano and the deepest, longest canyon in the solar system.

Jupiter

Did you know?

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system and is mostly made of hydrogen and helium.

It has the Great Red Spot, a massive storm that has been raging for centuries.

Saturn

Did you know?

Saturn is famous for its stunning ring system, made mostly of ice particles and rock debris.

It is the second-largest planet in the solar system and has at least 82 moons.

Uranus

Did you know?

Uranus is unique because it rotates on its side, making its axial tilt 98 degrees.

It is an ice giant with a methane-rich atmosphere that gives it a blue-green color.

Neptune

Did you know?

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun in our solar system.

It has the strongest winds in the solar system, reaching speeds of up to 1,500 miles per hour.
